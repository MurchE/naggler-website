How it works

Three clicks a day.
Nothing to learn.

The whole loop is small on purpose. Naggy lives in the tray, comes by a few times an hour, and gets out of your way. You can be running him inside of sixty seconds.

01

Set what matters today

Type your top 3 priorities. Drag to rank. That's the whole setup. You can ask Naggy to help rank them if today feels fuzzy.

02

Get gently redirected

Every few minutes Naggy peeks in. Four buttons — your three priorities or "other." One click. He's gone. Under two seconds.

03

End the day clear

Naggy shows you where your time actually went. If something was off, you noticed in the moment — not in a postmortem a week later.

Talk to Naggy

A little AI under the hood — pointed the right way.

Naggy isn't just a button. You can talk to him in plain English. He helps you rank what matters, breaks fuzzy goals into bite-sized tasks, and — when you're frozen — suggests the next smallest thing you can actually do.

  • "Help me put first things first." He looks at your top 3 and asks the one sharp question that re-ranks them.
  • "Break this down for me." A vague "write the proposal" becomes four concrete steps you can click through.
  • "What should I do right now?" Given your day so far and what's on the list, he picks a bite-sized next move.
